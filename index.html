About Tokimura

1928: A Sanctuary
for Your Breath.

Built in 1928, this house preserves the traditional Japanese way of life, its spaces gently divided only by fusuma doors and shoji screens. This is not a place for socializing, but a sanctuary to indulge in the luxury of solitude.

The soft light filtering through the shoji, the subtle scent of tatami mats, and the overwhelming silence of the night. Here, taking off your watch and simply listening to the sound of your own breath will become the most precious experience of your modern journey.

We named this inn "Tokimura"—literally translating to "Village of Time." First, to be your basecamp for traveling through the immense layers of time accumulated in the town of Mikuni. Second, to provide a space where you can focus solely on the shifting signs of the day and seasons, returning to your own inner stillness.

Five Rooms, Five Fragments of Time

Built in 1928, this house holds five rooms, each harboring a distinct narrative. Designed in the traditional style, with spaces gently divided only by fusuma doors and shoji screens, guests share the spacious kitchen and bathroom, creating a single, peaceful dwelling together.

We also offer complimentary rental bicycles to support your exploration. Ride towards the sacred, uninhabited Oshima Island while feeling the sea breeze, or take your bicycle on the local train to venture into the sanctuaries of Zen and the villages of master craftsmen. It is a modest gift to help you navigate the flow of time running through the deep layers of Mikuni and Fukui, more profoundly and freely.

A Remaining Miracle

Saved by Being
Forgotten.

During the Meiji era, when Japan's railway network was being established, Mikuni was left out of the main logistics routes. Having escaped rapid modernization and the fires of war, its dignified streets and unhurried flow of time remain untouched today.

"Being inconvenient served as a protective barrier, a sanctuary to preserve the history of this town."

Access

From Kyoto

(Approx. 2.5 hours)

Take the Limited Express "Thunderbird" to JR Fukui Station. Transfer to the Echizen Railway (Mikuni-Awara Line) and disembark at Mikuni Station. *Note: Be careful not to confuse this with the final stop, "Mikuni-Minato Station." The house is a 10-minute walk away. I highly recommend a route through the atmospheric old streets of the Kitamaebune era.

From Kanazawa

(Approx. 1h 30m)

Take the JR local train or Shinkansen to JR Awaraonsen Station. Board the Keifuku Bus (Route 85 or 97) and alight at Eishoji (Tatemachi), a stop in the heart of the historic district. This cost-effective and scenic route offers a slower transition from the city’s pace to our quiet harbor.

From Takayama

(Approx. 3.5 – 4 hours)

Take an express bus (Nohi Bus) from Takayama to Kanazawa Station. From Kanazawa, follow the coastal route via JR Awaraonsen Station as described above. This "Mountains to Sea" route connects Japan’s mountainous heart to its historic coastal gateway.
